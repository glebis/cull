# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cull is for AI artists, creative technologists, designers, researchers, and agentic workflow builders who manage large local archives of AI-generated images, references, prompts, metadata, ratings, selections, and collections. They often review hundreds or tens of thousands of assets in focused desktop sessions on macOS, with keyboard-heavy workflows and a strong preference for keeping their archive under local control.

## Product Purpose

Cull is a local-first, keyboard-first, agent-native image curation tool for AI-generated creative work and visual reference archives. It turns chaotic folders of images and generation metadata into coherent collections that humans and AI agents can search, review, compare, organize, export, and publish. Success means users can move quickly from visual clutter to decisions, collections, and reusable creative memory without hidden cloud dependency or destructive changes to source files.

## Brand Personality

Cull should feel precise, calm, technical, culturally literate, and privacy-aware. The product voice is direct and confident, with enough editorial sensibility to respect art, publishing, memory, and visual culture. It should feel like a serious creative tool rather than a generic productivity dashboard or AI novelty app.

## Anti-references

Cull should not look or sound like generic SaaS, corporate AI boosterism, privacy fear marketing, cute onboarding, or vague productivity software. Avoid decorative gradient blobs, glassmorphism, rounded marketing cards everywhere, clinical feature grids, noisy animations, and copy that explains obvious UI mechanics. The app UI should not dilute its terminal-inspired, monospace-first visual system with unrelated type, color, or component patterns.

## Design Principles

1. Keyboard flow is primary. Every major interaction should be fast, predictable, and reachable without a mouse.
2. The image archive is the main content. Chrome should help users inspect, compare, and decide without competing for attention.
3. Local-first trust must be visible in the product experience. Privacy and data ownership should be clear without becoming alarmist.
4. Agent-native behavior should feel like infrastructure, not a novelty. MCP, CLI, and deep links should share the same mental model as the GUI.
5. Curation language should be concrete. Labels, errors, empty states, and confirmations should tell users what changed, what is safe, and what action is available next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Cull should target WCAG 2.2 AA for the desktop app wherever web UI semantics apply, with particular attention to keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable contrast in the dark Tokyo Night theme, non-color-only status indicators, reduced-motion support, and clear copy for destructive or privacy-sensitive actions. The app should remain usable for color-blind users and for users who scale text or navigate with assistive technology.
