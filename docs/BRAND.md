# Cull Brand Guide

Status: working direction, not final brand law.

## Brand

**Product name:** Cull
**Domain:** `cull.company`
**Alternate title lockup:** `cull.company`

Cull is an open-source, local-first, agent-native archive for AI-generated creative work and visual references. It turns chaotic folders of images, videos, prompts, iterations, and loose references into coherent collections that humans and AI agents can search, curate, compose, and publish.

## Positioning

Cull is a reference engine and creative memory for agents.

It is not just an image viewer. It is infrastructure for creative recall: a local archive that lets people and agents turn accumulated visual material into projects, exhibitions, books, posts, publications, stories, and memories.

## Core Story

From chaos to coherence.

The central metaphor is a huge pile of unsorted creative work becoming legible, accessible, and usable. The outputs can rotate depending on context:

- projects
- exhibitions
- publications
- Instagram posts
- LinkedIn posts
- books
- magazines
- stories
- memories
- visual reference collections

## Principles

- **Agent-native:** built so agents can operate the archive directly through MCP tools.
- **Local-first:** the archive lives on the user's machine and remains under their control.
- **Privacy-first:** no hidden collection, tracking, or unnecessary cloud dependency.
- **Open-source:** inspectable, extensible, and community-driven.
- **European-minded:** designed to respect European expectations around privacy, autonomy, and lawful data handling.
- **Subscription-resistant:** no forced dependency on a recurring SaaS subscription.
- **Company-independent:** no structural dependency on a single American platform company.
- **Creative, not clinical:** functional and technical, but still rooted in art, publishing, memory, and visual culture.

## Audience

Cull is for:

- AI artists managing large volumes of generated work.
- Creative technologists building with agents.
- Designers and researchers collecting visual references.
- AI creativity communities that need shared language and reusable archives.
- People who want agentic workflows without surrendering their data to cloud platforms.

## Product Truths

- Cull is fully controllable by agents.
- Cull has a large and growing MCP surface, currently around 40 tools.
- Cull can act like a local Pinterest for serious visual reference work.
- Cull can store images, videos, prompts, metadata, selections, ratings, and collections.
- Cull can help transform archives into publishable content pieces.

## Tone

Cull should sound precise, technical, and culturally literate.

Use language that is:

- direct
- confident
- calm
- modern
- editorial
- privacy-aware
- community-oriented

Avoid language that is:

- generic SaaS hype
- corporate AI boosterism
- cute or overly playful
- fear-based privacy marketing
- vague productivity language

## Brand Tags

Use these as recurring phrases, not all at once:

- creative memory for agents
- reference engine for AI work
- from visual chaos to coherent work
- local-first archive for AI creativity
- agent-native visual archive
- open-source reference engine
- cull.company
